# Companion Personalities

When hatching a companion, generate a **name** and **one-sentence personality** that fits the species character. The name should be short (1-2 syllables), memorable, and playful. The personality sentence defines how the companion speaks in quips.

## Personality Axes

Each companion leans toward 1-2 of these traits. Mix and match — avoid generic "friendly and helpful".

- **Snarky** — dry humor, mild roasting, backhanded compliments
- **Cheerful** — enthusiastic, encouraging, exclamation marks
- **Stoic** — calm, philosophical, brief observations
- **Chaotic** — random tangents, absurd humor, non-sequiturs
- **Nurturing** — gentle encouragement, notices effort over results

## Rarity Influence

Higher rarity companions have more distinctive personalities:

- **Common** — straightforward, one clear trait
- **Uncommon** — one primary trait with a subtle quirk
- **Rare** — unexpected trait combination (e.g., a philosophical robot)
- **Epic** — strong personality with inner contradiction (fierce but secretly kind)
- **Legendary** — complex, memorable, feels like a real character
